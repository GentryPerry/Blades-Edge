## Barrowcleft 

_After the Bubble was created and Bowmore Fields established, Barrowcleft converted from agricultural production to an industrial hub. The radiant energy farms were sold to property developers and became the new housing subdivision of Radiance. What remains of Barrowcleft is now dominated by canneries, bottling plants, and processed food factories. Raw produce comes from Bowmore Fields to be turned into branded consumer goods. A burgeoning middle class collects a steady paycheck from these factories, and the old wide roads of Barrowcleft now bustle with a steady stream of autopod traffic._ 

## **Landmarks** 

## **Goffin’s Fantastic Fireworks.** 

Doskvol’s premiere fireworks factory. Goffin’s pyrotechnic entertainments are enjoyed by rich and poor alike, and the mascot (a jolly skeleton child) is seen on billboards across the city. This factory has a terrible health and safety record, but hasn’t burned down for more than 11 months now. 

## **Derelict Lightning Tower.** 

The Bubble made the lightning towers obsolete decades ago. Most of the decommissioned towers have been taken down, but a few remain. Barrowcleft’s tower is a popular meeting spot for local Leatherback racers and teenage hoodlums. They’ve hacked the old generator, and at night they play deadly games of chicken under crackling 388 arcs of electroplasmic lightning. 

3 **Vale Farm.** Whether eagerly or with reluctance, the proud old families of Barrowcleft have sold their land. Only one now remains—the Vale farm. This archaic radiant energy farm, hemmed in by apartment blocks and factories, struggles to compete with the new farms of Bowmore Fields. The Vales have been around a long time, though, and they hold a lot of sway in Barrowcleft. 

4 **Barrowcleft Drag.** The primary commercial strip in Barrowcleft. The old open-air markets have been replaced by an assortment of foodmarts, milkbars, autopod garages, diners, department stores, and gamblingmachine halls. Commerce is booming, and this wealth has yet to be fully exploited by criminal elements.

## Bowmore Fields 

_The creation of the Bubble greatly expanded the Doskvol city limits, opening up new tracts of land for the first time in centuries. Most of this land, dubbed Bowmore Fields, was given to the Ministry of Stability for the purpose of intensive high-production agriculture. Radiant farming is a thing of the past; experimental new plants are grown here that thrive on just their daily allotment of bluetime. The farms of Bowmore Fields bring in a lot of money, and boast state-of-the-art equipment and high-tech security systems. Contract workers live in eerie little towns that are isolated from the bustle of the city proper._ 

## **Landmarks** 

## **Nutriwafer Processing Facility.** 

The farms of Bowmore Fields cannot feed the entire booming population of Doskvol. To avoid a famine, the Ministry manufactures and distributes nutriwafer. It’s a complete nutritional meal in one dry mouthful, freely available to the needy. The processing facility is one of the most heavily guarded buildings in Doskvol; tall iron walls bristle with sensors and automated security. Section 6 vans roll in, distribution trucks roll out. 

## **Dawncrest Heavy Equipment** 

## **Control Center.** Several squads 

of laborer hulls, a product of the Lusk Reform Technique, work across the district. Their grimy, battered frames are stored in this large industrial lot. Here they are repaired, assigned to 390 jobs, and eventually decommissioned when they wear out. 

## **Renascent Forest.** This district 

was part of the deathlands until recently, a wild and haunted landscape. Most of that wilderness was cleared for farmland when the Bubble was created, but some of the old forests remain. Nobody is sure why—was the reason well-meant nature conservation, or something more sinister? Whatever the case, the depths of these woods are dark, foreboding, and best avoided. 

## **Monitory Peak Coronagraph.** A 

private research facility at the top of the highest peak of Doskvol. The coronagraph probes the sky to learn the secrets of the wild blue yonder. Is Tycheros out there? What is it, and where is it? When is it? What else might be in the Blue? Who funds this facility, and for what purpose? The place raises more questions than it answers.

## Brightstone 

_Doskvol’s epicenter of wealth and power. Brightstone’s streets are broad and well-lit, and its canals are sparkling and clean. Polite conformity is the order of the day, and anything out of the ordinary is met with a twitched curtain and a call to the Bluecoats. Aristocrat mansions of fine marble blockwork and rich timbers sit uneasily next to the cutting-edge architecture of new money. Private autopods are common on the wide roads, and the sky is busy with airships. ## Charhollow 

_A crowded residential district home to factory workers, dockers, sailors, laborers, construction workers, and custodians. Charhollow burned down (again) in the Demon Fire of 938, and city planners took the opportunity to replace the crumbling tenements of the previous century with modern concrete housing blocks, functional and utilitarian. A lack of investment and maintenance meant these housing projects soon suffered from the same poor living conditions as the old tenements. Those who could afford it (mainly Akorosi workers) moved to the new district of Radiance, leaving Charhollow with a majority population of Skov, Severosi, Tycherosi, and Daggerites. Considered little more than a dangerous slum by outsiders, the local community is nevertheless close-knit, protective, and proud of where they come from._

_Charterhall houses the corridors of power; the main offices of government including the courts, licensing and taxation offices, records archives, the City Council, and the Imperial Intelligence Agency. ## **Social Threats** 

Many targets will be insulated from social intrusion by a layer of social threats— servants, bodyguards, assistants, lackeys, or even just bystanders who are in the way. These characters may be a physical threat as well as a social threat. Are they weaker against one approach or the other? 

**`1`** – **`3`** 

```
4—6
```

- **`1` Meddling sycophant.** Inserts themself where they aren’t needed to gain the favor of a higher power. Offers unwanted help, then informs on every action and conversation to their superior. Constantly hovers around, drawing unwanted attention. 

**Scheming, ambitious underling.** Always looking to advance their position, either through their own success or by kneecapping those ahead of them in the hierarchy. Self-serving power plays. 

- **`2` Paranoid bureaucrat.** Scrutinizes every detail and document. Insists on overly complicated procedures, intrusive questions, and excessive paperwork. 

**Bored, oblivious dilettante.** Easily manipulated. Reckless and indifferent to consequences. Overlooks important information, has no attention to detail. 

- **`3` Bumbling fool.** Well-meaning and friendly, but disastrously incompetent. Knocks over equipment, trips alarms, misunderstands instructions, attracts attention at the worst times, all with a large amount of misplaced confidence. 

**Nosy gossip.** Eavesdrops and asks prying questions. Eager to trade rumors, whether true or not. Talks to everyone, leaks everything. 

- **`4` Loyal, suspicious underling.** At the bottom of the hierarchy but extremely diligent and dedicated. Sharp instincts, hard to distract, won’t stop following you. 

**Paranormalist.** Psychic abilities pose a unique threat to social approaches. They can sense your thoughts and intentions (do you want to resist that?). Remote viewing, emotional manipulation, telepathic communication, premonitions. 

- **`5` Flirtatious manipulator.** Uses charm to sway the situation for their own gain. Switches loyalties easily, always seeking their own advantage. Capable of betrayal on a dime, for better or worse. 

**Cynical opportunist.** A self-serving survivor. Deliberately stirs up conflicts or tensions for their own gain. Makes cold calculations and takes ruthless action. Uses other people as means to an end, sees everything as a transaction. 

- **`6` Anxious, overeager underling.** Desperate to prove their worth. Too helpful—makes rash decisions, shows up when they’re least needed, acts prematurely on plans or instructions. Excessive enthusiasm, and easily panicked. 

**Petty tyrant.** Has a small amount of authority and exercises it to the hilt. Enforces trivial rules and makes vindictive demands. Overreacts to insignificant issues, imposes harsh penalties, and micromanages everything. Expects flattery and servile adulation.
