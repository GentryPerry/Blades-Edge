## Geopolitics of the Shattered Isles 

## **Akoros, the Imperium** 

A land of dark, petrified forests and rocky hills. 

Formerly the heart of the Imperium; now the Imperium consists of little else. Although Akoros has lost its total grip on world power, it is still rich and powerful, enjoying the fruits of centuries of colonization and imperialism. The people are a conglomeration of cultures that have grown together in close proximity for centuries, along with many diverse immigrant communities. 

**Political Goals:** Still a dominant superpower, locked in a cold war against the United Skov Covenant. Seeks to contain Skov ideology (which emphasizes an ancient paranormal tradition that Section 6, and the Crown, strongly denounce), manipulate foreign governments to maintain its own power, and safeguard Imperial technological and military superiority.

## **The Dagger Isles** 

A tropical archipelago covered in dense jungle growth. 

The Dagger Isles are a region of small nation-states known as banyans, which include Biringan, Lhan Lhaban, Melamati, Xis̄ra, and many others. The Imperium and the USC fight many of their proxy wars via the Dagger Isles, covertly supporting revolutionary and counterrevolutionary groups, and sending in troops to tip the balance when needed. Some of the banyans are little more than puppet regimes as a result, but others remain (as they always

Others are already aligned with either the USC or the Imperium. For example, while Biringan is technically independent, its corrupt government is firmly under the thumb of Akoros. They focus on suppressing political dissidents and maintaining their hold on power.

## **Iruvia** 

A land of black deserts, obsidian mountains, and raging volcanoes. 

Iruvia was the first nation to secede from the Imperium, and later fought to support the secession of Skovlan. That war ended with the creation of the Null Zone, wiping out both South Point in Akoros and Bright Harbor in Iruvia. Since then, Iruvia has entered an isolationist period, only allowing a small amount of trade with the outside world via Mistport and the Dagger Isles. 

**Political Goals:** Iruvia would like to limit the expansion of both the Imperium and the USC, and sometimes plays them against each other. Ultimately, though, they want to avoid another Unity War at any cost. Their intelligence agencies are small but vigilant, focused on sovereignty, counterintelligence, and preventing their territory from being used as a pawn in someone else’s game.

## **Severos** 

A land of windswept plains, covered in dark, thorny scrub. 

Severos was the first to join the Imperium and although it is now an “independent protectorate,” the tendrils of Akoros are deeply embedded. The ruling class send their children to Akorosi schools, and Akorosi corporations extract the profit from the nation’s factories, farms, and vast fields of psychoactive deathlands flora (a key resource for pharmaceutical giants like Xantara Pharm). 

The indigenous people of Severos are proud horse-riding spirit-hunters, who built warded bastions to protect their communities from ghosts. Despite centuries of colonization attacking these traditions, many remember the old ways. The indigenous Targish people rose up in Cullfield, fighting for radical redistribution of wealth, and founded the Targ Republic (still not officially recognized by the Imperium). There was also open revolution in Irondale, until Akoros mobilized troops to “restore peace and security.” 

The ancient, warded bastions of Severos have a strong presence in the resonance field, and a strange relationship with the physical dimension. It is possible to enter them from parts of other nations, such as Akoros, using the resonance field. Imperial powers have exploited this to claim and then quarry the structures for the uncanny, valuable stone they are made of. Severosi activists within Akoros, such as Burning Orange, fight to reclaim these spiritual lands, and to receive reparations for the damage already done. 

**Political Goals:** The career diplomats in the Severosi consulate in Doskvol are primarily tasked with advancing trade interests and corporate investment in Severos. They pocket a healthy kickback from any such deals. Even so, some in the consulate have secret sympathies with radical separatist groups.

## **Skovlan, the United Skov Covenant (USC)** 

A land of cold mountains and rough tundra. 

Skovlan finally achieved independence with Unity War III, forming the United Skov Covenant. Local communities elect ruling covens, who send delegates to the High Coven in Lockport. 

Doskvol has a large population of first-, second-, and third-generations Skovs; they struggle with oppression, distrust, and conflicting loyalties. 

**Political Goals:** Despite dealing with the legacy of centuries of colonization, as well as the intergenerational physical and mental trauma caused by leviathan blood processing, Skovlan has quickly established itself as a world power. The USC works tirelessly to overtake its nemesis, Akoros. Their espionage efforts focus on manipulating Imperial politics, stealing technological secrets, and destabilizing Imperium-aligned regimes.

## **Tycheros** 

A far-off city-state built around huge, slowly shifting black-stone monoliths. A sprawl of floating platforms, travelator walkways, and monorail lines that weave through the air. Some say they have flying autopods in Tycheros. 

Tycheros is connected to the rest of the Shattered Isles by a Void Gate, a portal-like device that allows rail lines to pass through the resonance field. Academics have suggested that the Void Gate is more than simply an efficient mode of transport, and that Tycheros might exist in another time entirely, or a different dimension, or somewhere very far away—possibly even somewhere out beyond the Blue. Tycherosi are rudely called “Visitors,” and sometimes treated like space aliens. Any talk of demon blood is, of course, a relic of the superstitious past. 

**Political Goals:** Tycheros does not maintain an official consulate, and keeps a very low profile. Its absence is not the same as disinterest. There are Tycherosi diplomats in the city, who attend summits and trade liaisons, and they quietly advance Tycherosi interests—maintaining political neutrality, keeping Tycherosi technology secret, and ensuring that the Void Gates are kept operational.

## Weather & Seasons 

Historically, Doskvol is a cold, rainy, and windy city on most days of the year, famous for its thick fog. The B.L.U.E. Array has changed this—it creates sunlight and bright blue skies. The Bubble is permeable to weather, however, and although bluetime is certainly warmer and sunnier than the eternal night of yesteryear, tourists are often heard to complain that there are more gray days and rain in the city than they expected from the postcards. 

The insultingly named months of the Unity Era have been rejected, and in the absence of any commonly accepted alternative, people have reverted to the traditional six months of Mendar, Kalivet, Suran, Ulsivet, Volnivet, and Elisar. 

The north of Akoros, where Doskvol lies, experiences three seasons. 

- **Winter** (Elisar and Mendar). Frigid snowstorms during night, icy and gray during bluetime. 

- **Spring** (Kalivet and Suran). Windy and rainy during night, but often sunny during bluetime. During the latter half of Suran, there are even heatwaves during bluetime, and some call that period the “blue summer.” 

- **Fall** (Ulsivet and Volnivet). Stormy at night, crisp breezy days during bluetime. 

## _**Bringing It to the Table**_ 

Consider having your campaign start at the beginning of ’68, and keep loose note of the seasons as time passes (perhaps the season changes at dramatic moments—like when the crew advances in Tier). You start in winter, progressing into spring, blue summer, fall, and back into winter. Gesturing at passing seasons can help ground the action in some kind of reality. 

You might roll for weather at the start of personal business (or a score). This backdrop can also help ground the action and make it feel like a real place. _The GM tells everyone that’s it’s a rainy week during this personal business phase, and it gives the players something to color their scenes, struggling with umbrellas, coming inside dripping water, gutters overflowing._

## Crime in Doskvol ’68 

Doskvol has long been considered the vice capital of Akoros, and never has that been more true than now in ’68. 

The Age of Consensus was a promise of peace and prosperity for all—the price was keeping your head down, not complaining, playing your part. But the promise has always been a lie, and the more time goes, on the more the cracks show. Old class divisions still exist as strongly as ever, and all the luxury and benefits of the modern world flow to the elites, just as they always have. 

That simmering tension is starting to erupt into protests, counterculture, and rebellion. The authorities respond with reactionary crackdowns, and the tension increases. The status quo cannot last, the Consensus is its breaking point. 

This upheaval and unrest creates opportunities, and many are rushing to take advantage. All are labeled criminals by the establishment, but they vary widely in their means and motivations. 

At the top of the ladder are criminal factions so powerful they align more with the interests of the establishment than the common troublemaker—groups like the Unseen, or Strangford-Michter Financial. Alongside this is the growth of organized crime—sophisticated syndicates and crime families like the SCORPION Syndicate or the Gallo Family. 

At the street level, the counterculture has led to an explosion in drug use, which many factions are profiting from. Economic inequality, inadequate housing, and food scarcity sparks gang violence, while some political groups are willing to take militant action to advance their agenda, such as the Burn Corps and the Youth Action Movement (the Yammies).

## Imperial Civics 

The institutional power in the Imperium flows from the Crown downwards, but there have been several evolutions over the last century. 

- **The Immortal Crown.** More often simply “the Crown,” refers to the government as a whole in the abstract sense, and the nation state at large. Most people don’t believe there was ever an actual “Immortal Emperor”; it was just a fancy title for the head of state. Some believe that there was an Immortal Emperor but that he was assassinated many decades ago (which caused the dark years). Some conspiracy theorists even believe there always was an Immortal Emperor, and he yet lives, and still controls everything from the shadows. 

- **The Chamber of Voices.** In an effort to placate the general unrest between Unity War II and Unity War III, the Crown instituted a limited form of representative government. Elections are held every three years, at which time each district of each city of the Imperium elects a “Voice.” These Voices then move to Imperial City, to represent their communities in the Chamber of Voices. The voices organize into various factions or parties representing different political interests (People’s Unity, the Progressive League, the Iron Coalition, etc.). 

- **The First Speaker.** They wield most of the actual power of the Chamber— oversight of the military, security, and taxes, as well as Section 6, and they appoint a Premier to each city to oversee national interests there. Tradition dictates that after being appointed, the First Speaker enters the hallowed Sanctum of the Immortal Crown alone. Once a Voice becomes First Speaker, they always follow a similar program as their predecessors, no matter what ideological differences they previously espoused. Patriots champion this crossparty stability as another example of the fine character of the Akorosi people. 

- **The Premier.** A figure appointed by the First Speaker to oversee the city on their behalf, and to enact their policies. In charge of Section 6, the military, and security. 

- **The City Council.** Consisting of six elected members. They are not paid a salary, and elections are largely decided by who spends the most on advertising, so the seats are almost invariably filled by wealthy individuals. They determine wideranging bylaws and ordinances unique to the city—Doskvol, for instance, is the only city in Akoros where gambling is legal and unrestricted.

## Food in Doskvol ’68 

Bluetime, and the Bubble, have led to a significant transformation in food and food culture. The light of bluetime means that crops and livestock can be raised that weren’t possible before, and the creation of Bowmore Fields allows agriculture on a larger scale than ever. At the same time, a skyrocketing population has put major pressure on the agricultural supply chain. Food scarcity and famine is a real issue for particular socio-economic groups. 

## **Low Income** 

Although bluetime made a whole new world of agricultural production possible, it also disrupted or eliminated many traditional food sources for those on low incomes, including canal weed, water moss, several species of algae, and a majority of the city’s mushroom farms. 

The grocery pass is the Ministry of Stability’s solution to the immense strain that the food supply chain is under. This pass is only issued to those who meet a minimum income level, and it’s required to shop for fresh groceries. The Ministry argues that these passes ensure “consistent, predictable demand.” They only want people who can regularly afford to buy fresh groceries to buy them at all, to avoid any shortages caused by random spikes in demand. 

Instead, those in poverty are offered nutriwafer, a complete daily meal in one mouthful, distributed for free by the Ministry of Stability. If eaten irregularly, consumers have reported that they struggle with the dry mouthfeel and unusual taste. Studies have shown that with a consistent diet of only nutriwafer, the consumer grows accustomed to it, and some even learn to enjoy their daily allotment. This is another reason poorer citizens are encouraged to rely solely on nutriwafer for their nutrition. 

There’s a thriving black market for fresh food like strawberries, goat meat, apples, and lettuce in the poorer districts. Some of these illicit dealers distribute the food at cost, for the benefit of the community, while others charge exorbitant prices and turn a tidy profit. 

The grocery pass only limits access to grocery stores, and even poorer districts have diners and milkbars.

## **Middle Income** 

Those on middle incomes are issued a grocery pass and can access a relatively wide range of food. Good fresh food is still expensive, so canned foods make up a large part of the typical diet; canned soups, beans, and processed meats (typically goat, chicken, or rat). It’s common to eat pre-packaged frozen meals in front of the plasmovision set. 

International food has become popular in the last decade, with Akorosi versions of Severosi, Daggerite, and Iruvian cuisine made popular through magazines and cookbooks (typically unrecognizable to those who know the real thing, with substituted ingredients and tepid flavors). 

Restaurants are no longer the sole preserve of the wealthy, and there has been a boom in fast food and casual dining. 

## **Classic High Cuisine** 

For the aristocracy and those with old-money pretensions, Akorosi High Cuisine is the order of the day. The rising popularity of casual dining amongst the middle class has led to gourmet restaurants becoming an important form of conspicuous consumption and class status. 

This type of cuisine is typically a multi-course affair, heavy on seafood, shellfish, buttery sauces, and elaborate decorative displays made from fresh greens like asparagus, cucumber, and salad leaves (not necessarily to be eaten, but as a mark of prestige and excess). Restaurant decor is typically dark wood paneling, velvet or leather seating, and muted lighting. Black tie dress is expected. 

## **New Cuisine** 

This trendy and experimental form of dining is popular among younger, newmoney professionals. It focuses on fresh ingredients, playful experimentation, and spectacular presentation. 

## _**Bringing It to the Table**_ 

Food is an evocative way to immerse yourself in the fictional world. Consider scenes set in restaurants, diners, or milkbars. Ask the players— what are you having? You can learn a lot about a character by what they eat or drink.
