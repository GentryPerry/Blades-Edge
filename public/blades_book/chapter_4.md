## The Paranormal 

he “paranormal” refers to all the weird, supernatural, and sciencefictional powers that exist in the world of _Blades ’68_ . It can be T anything from the ghost of a chimney-sweep haunting the basement to a tear in space-time that radiates the psychic experience of true collective consciousness. 

Much arcane knowledge has been lost to time, but every living thing in the Shattered Isles has an innate connection to the resonance field. This is a close parallel dimension where all psychic activity, thoughts, and emotions resonate. By focusing the mind, a human can **attune** to the resonance field to perceive partially into it, to sense the echoes of real-world events captured in the field, or to make a connection to the entities that dwell there. 

Some people see attunement as an exciting new area of human potential, a form of latent psychic power that could be unlocked in everyone. Others see attunement as a dangerous act that threatens the precarious stability of our reality. 

## _**What Happened to the Ghost Field?**_ 

If you’re familiar with _Blades in the Dark_ , you might be wondering what happened to the ghosts and the ghost field. The ghosts are still there, though now they’re called _echoes_ . There are fewer of them, thanks to the power of the Bubble and Section 6, but they are still the most common paranormal encounter in Doskvol. 

Why does the resonance field behave differently to the ghost field of old? Partly, this is a matter of perception; the ghost field is a strange “other world,” sensitive to the psychic attitude of those who perceive it. If someone expects it to be psychedelic, it will be psychedelic. Additionally, paranormal techniques are advancing, just as mundane technology is, and accelerating the rate of change. In other words, all the paranormalists, ministry scientists, and reality-criminals are the _cause_ of these weird anomalies and space-time fractures, even as they study and harness them. 

## **What Do Regular People Believe?** 

Any given Doskvol citizen’s experience and understanding of the paranormal varies widely. The Bubble has proved very effective at keeping the terrors of the deathlands out, Section 6 is more capable than ever thanks to technological advances, and their memory-wiping powers keep many paranormal incidents off the public record. That said, this is still a world where a cataclysm turned the fundamental rules of reality upside down, where the 

325 

psychic echo of a dead person will materialize from its body, and where the majority of the world’s population lives in protective bubbles to hide from the existential horrors that roam the wilds. 

There are four basic attitudes toward the paranormal: 

**Denialist:** Those who refuse to accept the paranormal at all, and wave it all away as having an ordinary scientific explanation. They cling to their willful blindness, encouraged by government censorship and propaganda. 

_Whatever you think you saw down there, you didn’t. Ghosts and goblins are children’s stories. Now close the door and I’ll make a cup of tea._ 

**Average:** The standard curriculum teaches children about the cataclysm, the dangers of reality instability, and the deathlands. Most people try not to think about this stuff too much, and any major paranormal events have probably been scrubbed from their memory. They will have heard of the resonance field, and might have even seen a resonance echo once or twice, but they feel safe inside the Bubble. 

_They call it an “echo,” this happened to my Aunt Klara. I’ll call Section 6, they’ll know what to do._ 

**Cutting-Edge Believer:** Those on the fringes of accepted science, who seek to categorize and understand beyond the limits of what the government wants you to know. They accept the existence of all manner of paranormal phenomena, but try to understand them in pseudo-scientific terms. They use **experiments** to replicate what was once achieved through superstitious ritual. 

_Sounds like a Class III Resonance Entity. An echo. I have a containment unit with me—take the children next door, I’ll sort it out._ 

**Old Believer:** Those who still understand the paranormal in antiquated terms. Maybe they’re eccentric, or they’ve learned from ancient tomes, or they’re just old. They see the world as a supernatural place, with broken gates of death leading to ghosts and demons and horrors plaguing these Shattered Isles. 

_That’s a ghost down there. Take my spiritbane charm and wait here—I know a whisper, she can help._ 

## **Paranormal Beliefs Across the Shattered Isles** 

Every nation of the Shattered Isles has its own approach to dealing with paranormal threats. 

- At the center of the **Iruvian** city of U’duasha is a powerful and ancient anomaly that they call U’du (“the Well”). This phenomenon draws resonance entities to it like a magnet, leaving the rest of the city untroubled. The Iruvians have invented artificial replicas of the U’du anomaly, which they use to protect all their cities. 

326 

- **Severos** has access to Bubble technology courtesy of its close relationship with the Imperium, but also has its own strong tradition of indigenous spirit-hunting. 

- The banyans of the **Dagger Isles** are protected by the Serpentine Paths. These light streams, which weave themselves through the entirety of the archipelago, drain resonance entities of their paranormal power, and even consume them entirely. Combined with the vivid bioluminescence of the archipelago’s flora and fauna, many Daggerites have no need for Bubbles. Banyans that have had a particularly long history of foreign invasion struggle, due to how disconnected they are from Daggerite traditions around spirits and paranormal phenomena. 

- The towns and cities of the **United Skov Covenant** are ruled by locally elected covens, who use a combination of traditional knowledge and advanced parapsychology to protect their communities from paranormal threats. 

In general, most people outside of the Imperium have a wider knowledge of the paranormal, and more experience of occult traditions, than your typical Akorosi citizen. They soon learn to keep this knowledge to themselves within the borders of the Imperium, where Section 6 is always watching for reality infractions. 

Despite all evidence to the contrary, Imperial propaganda asserts that rival nations want to destroy the protective Bubbles and steal the technology for themselves. 

## **The Paranormal and You** 

A close encounter with an echo or other resonance entity is a harrowing experience. By default, the standard effect is to either paralyze a person with fear or panic them into fleeing from its presence. A PC can choose to freeze or flee, or make a resistance roll with **Resolve** to ignore the effect. 

Being possessed by an echo **deadlocks a key** at the end of every week. Once the possessed body suffers its final deadlock, it can no longer sustain its life. The possessing echo must abandon it and seek out a new host or, if they perform the necessary occult rituals, bond with the corpse and become a vampire. 

Large anomalies or mind-bending horrors (such as the physical manifestation of a forgotten god) may have additional effects on sight (in addition to causing viewers to freeze or flee). You may choose to resist them, too, as normal. 

_When you behold the billowing, veiled form of the Red Queen, you stand frozen in place as she envelops you, and you feel a powerful drowsiness—an urge to sink into a sweet, suffocating, fiery sleep..._ 

Other than freeze or flight, other common responses to paranormal phenomena include nausea, disassociation, hallucinations, memory loss, and intense positive or negative emotions.

## **Practical Applications of Paranormal Science** 

Certain applications of paranormal science are widely adopted and government-sanctioned, found in every home and business. Many other applications are rare, illegal, and dangerous. 

**Sparktech.** Any device that utilizes electroplasmic circuitry, making it inherently paranormal. Advanced and complex electronic machinery. 

**Electroplasmic Fusion.** Electroplasm makes modern life possible, powering everything from the city-protecting Bubble down to every humble lightbulb. Electroplasm was once refined from leviathan blood, but today the world runs on electroplasmic fusion. This super-efficient process turns a deceased person’s psychic residue, their resonance echo, into raw electroplasmic power. Where once the dead were cremated to stop their echoes haunting the physical plane, now they are cycled through the fusion plant to contribute to the safety and prosperity of those still living. The body remains intact, and Section 6 disposes of it privately and respectfully. 

The secrecy around the Bellweather Fusion Station has led to a number of conspiracy theories. Anti-war activists often assert that the Imperium’s constant warfare (or “peacekeeping”) is really just to keep the fuel flowing. Soldiers are conscripted, die on the frontlines, and their bodies are sent back home in resonance-stasis to be disposed of properly at a fusion station in Akoros. 

**Plasmoleum.** Electroplasm is a raw current distributed to Doskvol’s power grid, and the fusion station generates the Bubble directly. Some portion of the power output is also refined into plasmoleum, a high-energy liquid with a viscous consistency and a sharp, pungent odor. This is sold wholesale and redistributed by several different retailers. Plasmoleum fuels autopods, as well as many other machines. 

**Ultraplasm.** Plasmoleum can be further refined into an even more potent fuel source. Ultraplasm is volatile, radioactive, and delivers a huge energy yield. 

**Alluvium.** The physical residue of the resonance field—quantum instability in physical form. Rare, unstable, and very expensive on the black market. This substance breaks the laws of reality by its very presence, and most advanced paranormal experiments require some measure of alluvium as a catalyst. 

## **Paranormal Entities** 

The Metaphysical Entity Classification System (Bethan, 946) is the most common way to identify and categorize the various paranormal entities. 

**Class I Resonance Disturbances.** A non-physical manifestation, such as spectral lights, voices and sounds, vapors, etc. 

**Class II Resonance Entities: Loopers.** A single psychic imprint captured in the resonance field, repeating over and over. It has no will of its own, but is 

328 

still dangerous because of the electrical discharge of its plasmic form. They sometimes spontaneously manifest at the site of an extremely violent event, whether or not death was involved. 

**Class III Resonance Entities: Echoes.** Resonance echoes are the most common and widely encountered paranormal entity. An echo is a person’s psychic impression in the resonance field, which will manifest in physical reality after the death of the body (unless the body is properly disposed of). Echoes destabilize over time, becoming more incoherent and disturbed. They’re drawn to possess victims in order to drain their resonance energy. The trauma of their condition leads them to seek vengeance upon those they believe wronged them in life. 

**Class IV Resonance Entities: Mirrors.** Some echoes stabilize into a more permanent form, becoming “reconciled.” Their mental faculties remain intact and they no longer crave the resonance energy of the living, as echoes do. Mirrors are extremely rare. 

**Class V Resonance Entities: Aberrations.** Also known as “horrors.” These entities are endemic to the resonance field, and should not exist in our reality—but post-cataclysm, they can and do manifest in the physical realm. They’re very rare within the city, but common in the deathlands outside the Bubble. Aberrations are intensely powerful and highly resistant to harm. They may only persist in this world for a short time, however. They cannot sustain life for long outside the resonance field and they inevitably decay to dust or ooze in the span of a few hours or days. 

See _Blades in the Dark_ , page 304, for a table to generate your own horrors. 

**Class VI Metadimensional Entities.** Traditionally called “demons.” The resonance field is the closest dimension to our own, but it is not the only one. Class VI Metadimensional Entities are inhabitants of distant and alien dimensions. Their native dimension must have some affinity with ours, and the Class VI Entity uses this conduit to manifest in our reality. Some are beings of pure exothermic reaction (flame and smoke), others comprise dynamic fluid systems (the sea), others are beings of the upper atmosphere and stellar systems (the sky and stars), and so on. 

Unlike humans, Class VI Entities are not echoed in the resonance field. They are corporeal creatures, though extremely difficult to kill. Most appear as terrifying humanoids with physical features related to their elemental affinity. Some can transmute into their corresponding element, and a few can disguise themselves with a convincing human appearance. 

Class VI Entities are alien cosmic entities that transcend human understanding. Their desires are vast, cruel, and incomprehensible. 

329 

Although their powers are as diverse as they are, every Class VI Entity possesses preternatural speed and strength, telepathic communication, powerful telekinesis related to their elemental affinity, and the ability to travel via their elemental affinity (such as between water sources, for an entity from a water-based dimension). 

A closely guarded secret is that a **sparkmind** , or so-called artificial intelligence, uses the same scientific principles behind hulls but applies them to Class VI Entities. A very risky scientific procedure can, in theory, capture such an entity in its most pure elemental form: water, fire, smoke, etc. This elemental form is then entombed within the construct, and an arcane pattern of resistors, capacitors, and circuits are used to bind and contain it, harnessing some fraction of its immense intellect for the creator’s use. The wisdom of doing so is questionable. 

## **Other Entities** 

**Hollow.** A living body without a resonance echo. When a hollow is produced by the Lusk Reform Technique, they are called “the Reformed.” Usually dimwitted and easily controlled, though there are rumors of hollows that have been trained with skills beyond normal human limits. 

**Hull.** A sparktech robot animated by a resonance echo. Note that most people don’t see resonance echoes as being intrinsic to their self-hood in the same way that a soul would be. The scientific consensus is that a resonance echo is just a psychic residue that accrues in the resonance field, and that its removal can calm violent or criminal impulses. Thus, most people view a hull as a utilitarian piece of hardware, no more alive or human than a toaster. 

**Possessed.** A parasitic echo attachment. When resonance echoes manifest in the physical plane, they crave the resonance energy of the living, and must possess them to consume it. Multiple echoes may inhabit one body. 

**Vampire.** An undying body animated and sustained by a resonance echo. When a parasitic echo bonds with its host body, via paranormal procedure, it becomes a vampire. The vampire’s own resonance echo diffuses fully into the physical body, giving it functional immortality while at the same time severing its link to the resonance field. 

Vampires have been exceptionally rare for centuries, and are even more endangered due to the invention of the B.L.U.E. Array. The sunlight of bluetime burns and destroys them, so they must take great pains to avoid it. 

A vampire sustains itself by feeding on resonance energy from a living being. In fables, vampires drank blood to accomplish this, but this is not typical. Each vampire feeds on energy in its own way—leaving a distinct telltale sign on its victim afterwards. A vampire can easily detect these signs in order to locate their victims again for feeding, or to take care to avoid feeding on the chosen prey of another vampire.

## **Anomalies** 

An anomaly is a tear in the fabric of reality, a rift through which paranormal entities enter our dimension, and where the fundamental laws of reality no longer hold true. Anomalies can appear in a range of magnitudes. 

**Minor Anomaly:** A thinning of reality in a particular location. The precursor to a proper tear. Often accompanied by uncanny sensations such as déjà vu, dizziness, or unusual temperatures. Paranormal activity is increased, and paranormal powers are heightened. 

**Standard Anomaly:** A room-sized tear in reality, a rift that opens directly to the resonance field (or possibly a more distant dimension). Causes strong disturbances in its vicinity, up to a distance of a city block or more. Greatly increased paranormal activity, multiple resonance entities, etc. 

**Major Anomaly:** A rift the size of a city block. Rare. The effects are felt districtwide, and are powerful in magnitude. 

**Mega Anomaly:** A tear in reality the size of an entire district, or even multiple districts. Very rare. Quite likely to be a portal to distant dimensions, or other locations, historical eras, or timelines. 

Some anomalies are off the scale, such as the cataclysm. 

## **Anomaly Distortions** 

Anomalies can cause a wide range of localized reality distortions. These effects may disappear if the anomaly is sealed, but they may persist—sometimes permanently. 

Anomalies can be stabilized and harnessed through paranormal experimentation, giving some measure of control over which distortions manifest through the anomaly, and how they manifest. 

**1** – **2** 

- **1 Gravity:** Super heavy gravity, less gravity, or no gravity at all. 

- **2 Time:** Short loops, long loops, glimpses of past or future, fast aging, reverse aging. 

- **3 Magnetism:** May cause metal objects to be crushed, fly together, etc. 

- **4 Dimensional portals:** May be a large semi-stable portal to another place or time, or may be multiple small portals connected around the local area. 

- **5 Temperature:** Sweltering heat, freezing cold, etc. 

- **6 Plant growth:** Rapid, tangled spread of plant life. 

332 

## **3** – **4** 

- **1 Tessellation:** Upside-down staircases, recursive paths, impossible geometries, etc. 

- **2 Emotions:** Induces an extreme emotional state in those nearby, such as rage, lust, euphoria, terror, etc. 

- **3 Light:** Psychedelic colors, bright light, complete darkness, etc. 

- **4 Sound:** Complete silence, or deafening loudness, or a random combination thereof. 

- **5 Spatial dimensions:** Spaces that make no sense—rooms too large for their exterior, etc. People or objects may be scaled at random. 

- **6 Memory:** Key memories may be altered or disappear entirely. 

## **5** – **6** 

- **1 Object permanence:** Physical objects, or even body parts, may phase in and out of reality, disappearing and reappearing at random. 

- **2 Genetic coding:** Rapid biological evolution. Physical mutations, whether subtle or radical. 

- **3 Weather:** Localized meteorological phenomenon. Hurricanes, thick fog, thunderstorm, freezing blizzards, etc. 

- **4 Probability:** Things that should not be possible become likely, or vice versa. Distortions of this kind have caused major problems in the casinos of Silkshore. 

- **5 Synaptic connection:** Telepathic communication and the emergency of collective consciousness. 

- **6 Entropy:** The degradation of matter and energy. Things rust, decay, and break down. Machines stop working.

## **Section 6 and the Death Radar** 

The **Death Radar** at Section 6 Central detects whenever someone dies in the city, and it sounds a klaxon. This is only heard by those near the site of death, and by the **Reality Wardens** , whose ceramic masks contain a special sparktech receiver attuned to the klaxon. 

A team of **mourners** , hollows driving an unmarked car, use a resonance counter to find the body. They park outside, resonance counter crackling and clicking, and radio the precise location to Section 6 Central. A Reality Warden is dispatched to retrieve the body and bring it to the Bellweather Electroplasmic Fusion Station for processing. 

After its resonance echo has been processed, the physical body is disposed of in a confidential but entirely respectful manner. 

Reality Wardens are also responsible for securing and sealing anomalies. After locking down the surrounding area, they use large sparktech devices called Stability Anchors to dampen the anomaly’s effects and seal it entirely. They usually wipe the memory of any local witnesses after the anomaly has been secured. 

## Paranormal Playbooks 

If your character dies, you may transfer to the **Ghost/Echo** playbook if you wish. As a PC who can resist any amount of harm, remember that your character’s death is unlikely to occur unless you want it to. If you want to try playing a resonance echo, you’ll have to help make it happen. 

A Ghost/Echo may eventually transfer to a **Hull** or **Vampire** playbook, if they manage to complete the tasks necessary to become one of those entities. Building your own hull is a complex sparktech project. Preparing a hollow body for a vampire involves finding, learning, and performing an arcane paranormal experiment. 

Sometimes you might introduce a **Time Traveler** , from either the past or the future. They come to ’68 for a specific purpose, and return to their own time after a short but eventful visit. 

If you’d like to begin the game as a paranormal playbook, rather than first playing a standard playbook, talk to your game group about the idea and see what they think.

## Ghost/Echo **A RESONANCE ECHO ADRIFT WITHOUT A BODY** 

**When you play a Ghost/Echo, you earn xp when you exact vengeance upon those whom you deem deserving, or when you express your outrage or anger.** Get payback on those who wronged you, but watch out—Reality Wardens will hunt you down, and paranormalists may attempt to bind you. 

**Starting Actions:** 1 Hunt, 1 Prowl, 1 Attune 

Add all your current action ratings to the ratings on the Ghost/Echo playbook. You may not have an action rating greater than 4. 

Remove any deadlocks, and consider changing your keys to reflect your new, vengeful disposition. Note down any iconic items you carried in life that still exist in your ghostly, glitched-out resonance form. Instead of contacts, note down only your enemies and rivals. 

You keep any Echo special abilities from your human playbook; _Echo Fighter_ , _Echo Walker_ , etc. You lose all other special abilities. You start with the first trait on your playbook: _Resonance Echo_ . You may also choose one more trait. (Ghost/Echoes may not choose _Possess_ as a starting trait.) 

_Note: The traits on paranormal playbooks are not special abilities, and cannot be taken by living characters by using a veteran advance._ 

## **Drain** 

Instead of Stress, you now take Drain. You have 9 Drain boxes. Whenever you would take Stress, take Drain instead. When fully drained, strike out a key but choose an **echo irregularity** instead of a deadlock as normal. You cannot turn keys. 

Echo Irregularities: Chaotic, Destructive, Furious, Obsessive, Territorial, Sadistic. 

## **Need** 

You no longer have a vice. Instead you have an intense **need** : resonance energy. To satisfy this need, possess a living victim and consume their life essence (this may be a downtime action). When you do so, clear half your Drain (round down). You cannot possess victims at first—you have to advance to earn the ability.

## **Rebirth** 

Being reborn as a vampire is a radical transformation. While the occult ritual or paranormal experiment may be short, there will always be a period of weeks where the vampire acclimates to their new existence. You shun your former associates and live in the shadows, familiarizing yourself with your new form, while instinctively gathering dark servants and new material possessions. Consider a time skip of a few weeks, or play a temporary character in the meantime. 

When the vampire returns, you have two Dark Servants and the Vampire items listed on the next page. Your former relationships, whether friends or rivals, mean little to you now. 

## **Feeding** 

Your vice is resonance energy, consumed from a living human. Use 1 downtime activity to **Hunt** prey and clear Stress equal to the highest die. When you feed, mark four ticks on your healing clock (this is the only way you can heal). _How do you feed? What telltale sign do you leave on your victims?_

## Time Traveler 

## **A VOYAGER VISITING THIS ERA ON AN IMPORTANT MISSION** 

You are a visitor from the future, here on an important mission. Your mind starts muddled from the time travel process. Every time you fill your playbook xp, you gain clarity—choose a new **Time Traveler trait** , _**and**_ a **versatile ability** from another playbook (reflecting your former life and experiences), _**and**_ take another **temporal glitch** , _**and**_ note down a new **detail of your mission** . 

**When you play a Time Traveler, you earn xp when you address a challenge with fearless conviction or lateral thinking.** Time Travelers are powerful, but they’re here for a specific purpose, and once their mission is complete (or even before), they must return to their era and leave the crew behind. _You burn brightly but briefly; make the most of your time here._ 

Unlike the other paranormal playbooks, an existing character does not typically transfer to this one. A player might wish to play a Time Traveler while their regular PC is in prison, or if they overindulge their vice and go on a bender, or if their character marks their last deadlock and has to retire. Alternatively, a player might want to mix things up and try the Time Traveler out, and their regular character simply sits on the bench while they do. There’s no right or wrong way to introduce the Time Traveler. 

**Starting Actions:** Place nine dots (max three dots in any action), reflecting your personal history, experience, background, and heritage. 

**Why this crew? Why crime?** All Time Travelers come to ’68 for an important mission, which might beg the question of why they choose to join a criminal crew and goof around on scores. You don’t have to answer this question right away, and should play to find out. But consider: 

- **The crew matter more than they realize.** The nature of campaign play, ticking timebombs, and the various playbooks means that the crew is almost always a pivotal piece in one or more world-changing events. 

- **They might be related.** Perhaps the Time Traveler is an ancestor or descendant of one or more PCs, or they were sent here by an ancestor or descendant. 

- **It might be random chance.** Perhaps the portal happens to open in the right place at the right time, and the Time Traveler has to make the best of the crew they’ve landed in.

## Faction Rolodex 

Each faction of Doskvol in 968AC is briefly described here, with detailed entries following for the criminal underworld, and the other most significant factions of the city. 

## Underworld 

**The Unseen** (VI): A highly organized and powerful group with secret membership. Thought to pull the strings of most of Doskvol. 

**SCORPION Syndicate** (IV): A massive and sophisticated Severosi drugsmuggling operation, mainly dealing in black lotus. 

**Strangford-Michter Financial** (IV): A powerful investment firm with a habit of backing illegal ventures off the books. Has stakes in a large number of criminal groups and shady scientific projects. 

**Mirror House** (III): Rogue scientists dedicated to the discovery and exploitation of metaphysical anomalies. 

**The Golden Hand** (III): A high-tech criminal organization, known for using gold-plated hulls to do its dirty work. 

**The Tomorrow Program** (III): A hedonistic association of notable scientists, writers, artists, performers, and dreamers. Lots of parties. Cover for extortion, graft, and murder. 

**The Gallo Family** (II): A tight-knit criminal enterprise with deep roots in Silkshore. Gambling, loan sharking, and narcotics trafficking. 

**The Keel Twins** (II): Identical twin sisters, ruthless gangsters, and minor nightlife celebrities. Many smaller gangs pay up to the Keels. 

**Lord Scurlock** (II): An old recluse. Possibly a vampire. Obsessed with paranormal secrets. 

**Silk** (II): A mysterious and legendary cat burglar, or possibly several of them. Every heist more ambitious than the last. Enjoys stealing the score from lesser thieves at the last moment. 

**The Blackjacks** (II): The remnants of the former Rail Jacks union, who turned to crime when their services were replaced by robotic hulls. Paranormal specialists and underworld muscle for hire. 

**The Burn Corps** (II): The radical militant arm of Burning Orange, the Severosi social activism collective. The Burn Corps is dedicated to armed self-defense, particularly against Bluecoat brutality. 

**The Limmerfield Gang** (II): A tough gang with a reputation for brutality and strangeness. 

**The Lounge Rats** (II): A seedy group of lounge lizards and nightclub owners. Extortion rackets, drug dealing, and illegal gambling. 

**Red Torque** (II): A tight-knit gang of elite racing drivers who use their skills for criminal ends. Ambitious, angry, and fast. 

**D-13** (I): Ex-soldiers with no future, forced to pursue criminal opportunities. 

**The Dirt Barons** (I): Freelance operators who dig up and trade dirt on other factions. Known for being honest brokers, they avoid crooked deals or double-crossing. 

**Leatherbacks** (I): Rough and rowdy autocycle racers. A youth subculture as much as a gang. Named for their ubiquitous colorful leather jackets. 

**Paxton** (I): A champion bare-knuckle boxer and a larger-than-life personality. Has spent more time inside Ironhook than out. Just got out on parole for the seventh time.
